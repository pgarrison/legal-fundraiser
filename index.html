Why is it so expensive?

I am a solo caregiver, now working two jobs (50-60 hrs a week) in order to provide the best life I can for lil A, but I need your help!

What’s up with this court case?

Having worked with many families involved in the child welfare system, and because I have already had a nephew in the child welfare system, I am keenly aware of the additional trauma that the system can unfortunately cause for kids and families. This is why I petitioned King County Superior Court for emergency guardianship & minor guardianship of lil A in March of this year.

After I became lil A’s emergency guardian and obtained her educational and medical records, I learned that she missed 51 days of school at day 98 in the school year, and had not been to a wellness child checkup in over 2 years, despite having repeated emergency room visits, and a hospitalization without follow up care in the last year. She came into my care with a baby tooth that had a missing filling that had fallen out and not been repaired, and shoes that were 2 sizes too small for her. As a mandated reporter in the state of WA I was required to call CPS and inform them of these facts. CPS did an investigation and determined that because lil A was safe and healthy at the present time in my care, they could close the case for the time being.

The ongoing minor guardianship case open with King County Superior court is the result of my petitioning to be lil A’s longer term guardian because I have serious concerns about the likelihood that my sister will be well enough to properly care for lil A in the near future.

My sister and I were initially working together as a unified team, but unfortunately she succumbed to the pressure of generational traumas and reverted to repeating old unhealthy cycles. I am hoping for the best for her healing journey, but at a bit more distance now as I had to get a domestic violence protection order to protect myself and lil A from further harm. If, and only if, my sister is healthy enough to raise lil A again at some point in the future, I would support that transition because I know reunification can be healing for kids and parents. I currently have grave concerns and doubts about that potential in the near future, yet I still wholeheartedly hope my sister finds the healing she needs.

I remain steadfast in protecting lil A from experiencing further trauma, and will do everything I can to promote healing for my precious niece. She is only seven years old and has a chance to have a healthier childhood, unlike myself and so many others in my family. She now goes to school regularly, receives appropriate medical and dental care, and receives specialized mental health services so that she can begin her healing journey early on in life. She now has a loving community and friendships with similar aged peers, a whole social life she never had before.
